Adoption and the Law

Adoption is an institution of the law and is governed by the Child right act of 2003. As an adoptive parent, The law establishes a relationship and conveys on you all the rights and privileges of child care. This means that the parenting rights and duties are transferred to you and the child will become a legitimate member of the family and as such is prohibited to engage in any form of sexual intercourse and marriage between both parties (Adopter together with the legitimate family member(s) if any and the adoptee).

How To Apply

The decision to adopt is a huge step and sometimes people are faced with excessive challenges that may cause stress. We'll provide you with support every step of the way as you find out more on adoption.

Who Can Apply

We welcome adopters from all walks of life. Adoption rights are granted to adoptive parents coming from different backgrounds and have different life experiences, skills and qualities.
