Who Can Apply

Please note that the only legal way to adopt in Nigeria is to work with the respective state social welfare office (usually named the Ministry of Women Affairs and Social Development). Prospective adoptive parents should not attempt to process their adoption through local officials who may attempt to circumvent the legal process.

Nigerian courts, except courts in Lagos and Ogun states, only allow Nigerian citizens (dual citizens included) to adopt a child in Nigeria. If married, both members of a married couple must be Nigerian citizens, except in Lagos and Ogun states.

It is worthy to note that the institution of adoption is regulated by the Child Right Act of 2003. Consequently this Act stipulates all the rights, privileges and processes of adoption. That been said, adoption eligibility is considered along the following:

Age: An applicant must not be less than 25years of age and must be at least 21years older than the child. Hence a child of 5yeras can only be adopted by an applicant who is 26years and above.

Marital status: Where an applicant is unmarried, the applicant must provide evidence proving their age to be 35 years and above . However, if married, applicant must obtain consent from their spouse.

Note: The Court has the power to impose certain terms and conditions as it may deem fit while making the adoption order. Furthermore, the Law does not prohibit a single person from adopting a child of the opposite sex. However, same sex relationships are considered criminal acts and as such same sex couples are not allowed to adopt.
