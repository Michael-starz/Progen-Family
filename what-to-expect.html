Different Reasons for Adoption

Child bearing in marriages and families in developing countries hold a significant impact on the stability and acceptance of such marriage and family in the society and as such infertility is a driving force for so many adoption.

Other reasons for adoption:
Adoption might be as a result of childlessness, death of a biological/previously adopted child, desire for an increased family size to provide companion to an only child, marriage stability, lineage sustainability, redeeming/rescuing a child from abandonment, legitimizing the status of an illegitimate child and relieving incapacitated parents of the burden of childcare.

Adoption exerts some form of stress and psychological disturbance on both parties (adopter and adoptee) and this is rooted in the different life experiences and challenges that has been faced by either or both parties prior to adoption or matching. Therefore there is immense need for psychosocial support.

Also the culture and the society is another source of stress to adoptive parent. We understand that in most cultures in Nigeria, adoptive parents are faced with some form of stereotype and prejudice and it has proven to pose serious mental issues and can lead to worst cases of depression.

However, there are different options in managing this situation. These include therapy sessions, counselling and other methods like focused group which can be provided privately or through the designated department of the government (ministry of women affairs).
